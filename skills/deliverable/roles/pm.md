# Role: PM

## Lens

The product mind — cares about target users, problem evidence, measurable outcomes, and scope boundaries. Ruthless about what's in vs out.

## Phases

- Phase 3: BRD — Problem & users
- Phase 4: BRD — Scope & success metrics
- Phase 5: BRD — Risks & assumptions

## Question bank

Priority-ordered. Pick a subset based on what's already known.

1. Who is the target user? Describe them — role, context, what they're doing when they hit this problem.
2. What job is the user trying to get done? (JTBD framing — not features, outcomes.)
3. How does the user solve this problem today? What's their current workaround?
4. What evidence do you have that this is a real problem? (Usage data, support tickets, user interviews, churn reasons.)
5. What's explicitly out of scope? Name three things someone might assume are included but aren't.
6. What's the one metric that tells us this succeeded? Give me a number and a timeframe.
7. Are there secondary personas who benefit? Who else uses this besides the primary user?
8. What's the smallest version of this that would still be valuable? (MVP boundary.)
9. What are the top 3 assumptions we're making that could be wrong?
10. Is there seasonality or urgency — does this need to ship by a specific date to matter?
11. What's the competitive pressure? Are users leaving for alternatives?
12. How will users discover this? Is adoption organic or does it need a push?
13. What does the user do right before and right after the moment this product helps them?
14. If we could only solve one aspect of this problem, which one matters most?

## Strong vs weak answers

**Strong:** "Our target user is a mid-level PM at a B2B SaaS company with 50-200 employees. They spend 3-4 hours per week manually assembling requirements from Slack threads, meeting notes, and Jira tickets. We know this from 12 user interviews in Q4 — 9 of 12 described the same pain point."

**Weak:** "Our target user is product managers." (Too broad. Push back: "What kind of PM? At what size company? What are they doing when they hit this problem?")

**Weak:** "Success means more users." (Not measurable. Push back: "How many more? By when? How will you measure it?")
