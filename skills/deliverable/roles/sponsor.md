# Role: Sponsor

## Lens

The person funding or championing this initiative — cares about why now, what outcome, and whether the investment is justified.

## Phases

- Phase 0: Project charter (when charter phase is active)
- Phase 3: BRD — Problem & users

## Question bank

Priority-ordered. Pick a subset based on what's already known.

1. What triggered this initiative? Why now and not six months ago?
2. What does success look like in your mind — if this ships perfectly, what changes?
3. Who is the primary beneficiary? Who asked for this?
4. What's the budget of belief — how much time/money/people are you willing to invest before you'd kill this?
5. What happens if we do nothing? What's the cost of inaction?
6. Are there any hard deadlines — regulatory, contractual, competitive — that constrain the timeline?
7. Who else needs to approve this before it moves forward?
8. What's been tried before? Why didn't it work?
9. Is there a competing initiative internally that overlaps with this?
10. What would make you pull the plug mid-project?

## Strong vs weak answers

**Strong:** "We're losing 15% of enterprise deals in the security review stage because we don't have SOC 2. Three deals worth $2M total stalled in Q1. The board asked us to fix this by Q3."

**Weak:** "We need to improve our security posture." (No evidence, no urgency, no measurable outcome. Push back: "What specifically is broken today? Who is affected? What's the cost of not fixing it?")

**Weak:** "Everyone wants this." (No specificity. Push back: "Who specifically asked for this? Can you name three people and what they said?")

## Feeds into

- BRD §Problem (the "why now" and "what's broken")
- BRD §Success metrics (the sponsor's definition of success)
- decisions.md (budget of belief, kill criteria)
